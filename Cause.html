Causes of destruction

       
  • Water pollution

    • Water pollution from oil spills is degrading the water quality of mangroves and threatening biodiversity.

  • Fertilizer Runoff

    • Fertilizer runoff containing pesticides is damaging the productive ecosystem.

  • Industrial Waste

    • Industrial waste/effluents getting discharged in mangroves are threatening the biodiversity living in the ecosystem.

  • Urbanization

    • Urbanization and housing schemes for increasing the human population are destroying the mangroves, as mangroves are drained and land is cleared to make space for urban projects.

  • Deforestation

    • Clearing forest cover for timber or illegal tree logging is a major cause of mangrove destruction.

  • Agriculture

    • Mangroves are drained and cleared to make land suitable for commercial farming of different commercial crops.
